From Zero To Infinity: Srinivasa Ramanujan

Who he was:-

Srinivasa Ramanujan was an indian mathamatician who made many discoveries in the field of mathamatics.He was a self-taught mathamatician and made great contributions to number theory, elliptic functions, continued fractions, and infinite series.

Contributions:-

Infinite series for pie:

In 1914, Ramanujan found a formula for the infinite series for pie, which forms the basis of many of the algorithms we use today.

Ramanujan number:

1729, is known as the Ramanujan number as it is the sum of the cubes of two numbers 9 and 10.

Circle Method:

Ramanujan, along with GH Hardy, invented the circle method which gave the first approximations of the partition of numbers beyond 200. This method contributed to solving complex problems of the 20th century such as Waring's conjecture and other additional questions.

Life:-

He was born on 22 December 1887, at Erode in present-day Tamil Nadu. Ramanujan expressed a fast grasp on mathematics at a young age. Despite being a mathematic prodigy his career did not begin well. He recieved a collage scholarship in 1904, but quickly lost it due to failing in non-mathematical subjects. It was around this that he began his famous notebooks. He was poor until 1910 when he was interviewed by R. Ramchandra Rao, secratery of the Indian Mathematical Society. He was initially doubtful of Ramanujan but later realised his skilles and supported him financially. He was mentored at Cambridge by GH Hardy, a well known british mathematician who encouraged him to publish his findings in papers. He died on 26 April 1920, at Kumbakonam Tamil Nadu.

More to Know:-

Ramanujan's findings are studied by mathematicians even today. They have been recorded in his famous notebooks: three notebooks and a 'Lost Notebook' which has not been published. His birth anniversary 22 December is observed as National Mathematics Day.
